Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers try to steal sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source.

In a typical phishing attack, a student may receive an email or message that looks like it is from a bank or a well-known company. The message usually contains a link asking the user to verify their account details. When the user clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks real. Once the user enters their login credentials or OTP, the attacker gains access to their account.

Students are often targeted because they frequently use digital platforms and may not always verify the authenticity of links. The consequences of phishing attacks include financial loss, identity theft, and misuse of personal data.

This case study highlights the importance of being cautious while using the internet and avoiding suspicious links or messages.
